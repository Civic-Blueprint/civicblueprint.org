# Civic Blueprint Website Phase 2 Brief

## Purpose

This document defines the Phase 2 direction for the Civic Blueprint website.

Phase 1 built the smallest credible public site to move from internal document development to external challenge. It shipped a proof-of-usefulness artifact, a readable framework presentation, and a contribution path. Its narrative was structured around a reform question: "what would have to change first?"

Phase 2 does not discard that work. It reframes it.

The formation-document analysis track in Project 2028 has surfaced an empirical finding that changes how the project should present itself: human civilizations across centuries, continents, and governance traditions converge on a remarkably consistent set of outcome commitments — dignity, accountability, broad participation, freedom, ecological responsibility. The methods differ radically. The outcomes they reach toward do not.

That convergence reframes the project. Civic Blueprint is not primarily asking people to adopt new values. It is surfacing alignment that already exists and asking why systems have drifted from commitments their own founding texts enshrine.

Phase 2 pivots the website narrative from reform framing to alignment framing.

## What Phase 1 Accomplished

Phase 1 delivered:

- a legible public entry point for serious outsiders
- a comparative proof-of-usefulness artifact (housing permitting + AI governance)
- readable web versions of the core framework documents
- a contribution path via GitHub issues and a plain-language form
- the blueprint visual identity and design system

Phase 1's narrative was problem-first: here is what is broken, here is a framework, here is a concrete test, challenge it. That narrative was appropriate for the project's state at the time. The framework needed external pressure before it could credibly claim broader relevance.

## What Changed

The formation-document analysis track (`project-2028/formation-docs/`) assembled a corpus of twenty-one formation documents — constitutions, charters, declarations, and organizational founding texts — spanning six continents and three centuries. Each was mapped against the project's 17 principles using the alignment framework.

The finding: convergence is the pattern, not divergence.

- Dignity appears in nearly every document.
- Accountability and constrained power appear in nearly every document.
- Some form of "the powerful must answer to those they govern" appears in nearly every document.
- Social provision, participation, and pluralism recur across radically different cultural and historical contexts.

This is not Civic Blueprint projecting its values onto the world. It is the world's values being surfaced and connected. The formation-document analysis provides empirical evidence that the alignment the project is built on already substantially exists — written down, ratified, and defended across centuries — but has never been named as a unified pattern or operationalized across contexts.

That finding changes what the project is asking of people. Phase 1 asked: "pressure-test our framework." Phase 2 asks: "look at what your own founding texts already say — and then ask why your systems aren't doing it."

## Phase 2 Strategy

### Narrative pivot

Phase 1 narrative: **problem → principles → proposals**

Phase 2 narrative: **shared aspiration → alignment evidence → principles as outcome targets → proposals as realignment moves**

The entry point shifts from "what's broken?" to "what do we actually agree on?" The answer — backed by the formation-document corpus — is: more than we think. The proposals stop being contested policy ideas and become the obvious next steps to close the gap between where we are and where our own commitments already point.

### The meta-thesis

The Phase 2 site should communicate a thesis that the project has been building toward but has not yet named:

1. Across time and cultures, human civilizations consistently reach toward the same outcome commitments: dignity, accountability, fairness, freedom, participation, ecological responsibility.
2. This convergence is not coincidence. It reflects a shared aspiration rooted in who we are.
3. Our systems have never fully embodied these commitments. The gap between what we've written and what our systems deliver is the problem space.
4. The work is not to invent new values. The work is to surface the alignment that already exists, name the drift, and coordinate to close the gap.
5. When people see that their commitments are shared, the sense of possibility shifts from "this is idealistic" to "this is overdue."

### Theory of change (recursive uplift)

The site should make legible a specific theory of change:

1. **Surface the alignment** — show that diverse civilizations already converge on shared outcome commitments (the formation-document analysis does this)
2. **Name it explicitly** — give people the language to see that their disagreements are mostly about methods, not outcomes (the principles do this)
3. **Build belief** — when people see that their commitments are shared, coordination becomes possible
4. **Act on it** — proposals become tractable because they are realignment moves toward outcomes people already endorse
5. **Results reinforce the alignment** — visible progress strengthens collective commitment, which enables harder moves

This is the recursive uplift: alignment enables coordination, coordination enables action, action produces results, results deepen alignment.

## What Phase 2 Is For

Phase 2 is for reframing the project's public identity around the alignment thesis while maintaining the challenge-oriented posture that Phase 1 established.

More specifically, it is for:

- communicating the alignment thesis as the project's central claim, backed by the formation-document evidence
- making the formation-document convergence finding visible and legible to new visitors
- reframing the principles as shared outcome targets that humanity has been converging on, not as Civic Blueprint's values being proposed
- reframing the proposals as realignment moves that close the gap between shared commitments and current system behavior
- preserving the challenge-oriented tone and contribution paths from Phase 1
- maintaining the blueprint visual identity and design system

Phase 2 is not for:

- abandoning the proof-of-usefulness approach or the framework presentation
- softening the epistemic posture or becoming promotional
- claiming the alignment thesis is proven beyond challenge
- launching a movement-style campaign

## Primary Audience

Phase 2 retains the Phase 1 primary audience and adds to it.

Retained primary audience:

- civic technologists
- public-interest product and operations people
- public administration and state capacity researchers
- policy-adjacent builders and institutional reform writers
- domain experts able to challenge the project's assumptions

Added Phase 2 audience:

- comparative constitutional scholars and political theorists
- people working on international cooperation, treaty design, or cross-border governance
- people who feel politically homeless — who hold strong commitments to dignity, accountability, and fairness but do not see those commitments reflected in any existing faction
- organizational leaders exploring alignment between their governance structures and broader civic commitments

Secondary audience remains:

- thoughtful non-specialists who can follow a plain-language summary and decide whether the project is worth deeper reading

## Product Principles

Phase 2 inherits all Phase 1 product principles and adds one.

### Inherited

1. Plain language at the entry point
2. Scannable writing
3. Objective tone
4. Accessibility from the start
5. Challenge, not applause

### Added

6. **Evidence before assertion**

The alignment thesis is a strong claim. The site should present the formation-document evidence before asking the reader to accept the interpretation. Show the convergence, then name the pattern. Let the evidence do the persuading.

## Core Message

The homepage should communicate five ideas quickly:

1. What if we are not as divided as we think?
2. Across centuries, human civilizations keep reaching toward the same commitments — dignity, accountability, freedom, broad participation.
3. This is not ideology. It is a shared aspiration rooted in who we are.
4. If we already agree on where we are going, why are our systems taking us somewhere else?
5. Let's close the gap — together.

## Homepage Evolution

### What stays

- Blueprint visual identity and design system
- Component architecture (panels, cards, grids, corner marks, typography)
- Challenge-oriented tone and contribution paths
- Proof-of-usefulness artifact and framework presentation
- Epistemic posture and provenance transparency

### What changes

The homepage narrative arc shifts. Phase 1 started with the problem. Phase 2 starts with the finding.

### Recommended Phase 2 section flow

1. **Hero**
   - Entry point reframed around the alignment finding
   - The question shifts from "what would have to change?" to "what if we already agree on where we're going?"
   - Epistemic posture preserved: this is a working thesis backed by evidence, not a settled claim
   - Primary calls to action: See the evidence / Read the first memo / Challenge this work

2. **The Finding** (new section)
   - Short, evidence-first presentation of the formation-document convergence
   - Across twenty-one documents, six continents, three centuries — the same commitments keep appearing
   - This is not Civic Blueprint's values. This is what humanity has already written down, ratified, and defended.
   - Visual: alignment matrix summary or convergence highlight (design TBD)

3. **The Gap** (replaces or evolves "Why This Exists")
   - If these commitments are shared, why don't our systems reflect them?
   - The problem is not that people disagree on dignity. The problem is that systems optimized for other objectives have drifted from commitments their own founding texts enshrine.
   - Link to the Problem Map for readers who want the full diagnostic

4. **Start With One Concrete Example** (retained, reframed)
   - The proof-of-usefulness artifact now reads as an example of the drift — a specific case where shared commitments (institutional competence, accountability) are not being met
   - Featured artifact remains the comparative housing + AI governance memo
   - Future artifacts may be added as additional examples of drift and realignment potential

5. **What The Framework Currently Says** (retained, reframed)
   - Principles presented as shared outcome targets, not Civic Blueprint's values
   - Problem Map presented as a diagnostic of where systems have drifted from shared commitments
   - Systems Framework presented as an analysis of how to close the gap
   - Process layer retained as-is

6. **The Alignment Evidence** (new section, or integrated into "The Finding")
   - Link to the formation-document analysis for readers who want to see the source-by-source evidence
   - Summary of the alignment matrix: which commitments are universal, which are strong, which are contested
   - Honest about what the evidence does and does not show

7. **What Kind Of Input Is Most Useful** (retained, expanded)
   - All Phase 1 input types preserved
   - Added: formation-document expertise, comparative constitutional scholarship, challenges to the convergence claim itself
   - "If you think this convergence is illusory, we need to hear that."

8. **How To Respond** (retained)
   - Same contribution paths
   - Same GitHub + plain form mechanism

9. **Closing** (evolved)
   - Not "does this framework help serious people see something more clearly?"
   - Now: "your own founding texts already say this. The question is whether we're ready to act like it."

## New Content Requirements

### Formation-document evidence presentation

Phase 2 requires a way to present the formation-document convergence finding on the web. This does not need to be the full analysis workspace. It should be a legible summary that:

- shows the breadth of the corpus (which documents, which traditions, which time periods)
- shows the convergence pattern (which commitments appear most consistently)
- shows the honest gaps (where sources diverge or where Civic Blueprint's principles go beyond the corpus)
- links to the full analysis for readers who want depth

This may be a new page, a section on the homepage, or both. Design and format are TBD.

### Alignment matrix web presentation

The alignment matrix (`formation-docs/analysis/synthesis/alignment-matrix.md`) should be presentable on the web in a way that is scannable and honest. This is the evidentiary backbone of the Phase 2 thesis.

### Updated framework framing

The existing framework pages (Principles, Problem Map, Systems Framework) do not need content changes. They need framing changes — introductory language that positions them within the alignment narrative rather than the reform narrative. The principles are shared outcome targets. The problem map is a drift diagnostic. The systems framework is a realignment analysis.

## What Phase 2 Does Not Change

- The 17 principles (content unchanged; framing evolves)
- The proof-of-usefulness artifact
- The contribution mechanism
- The blueprint design system and visual identity
- The epistemic posture (working claims, not settled truth)
- The challenge-oriented tone
- The technical stack (static Next.js export on S3 + CloudFront)

## Information Architecture

Phase 2 retains the Phase 1 page types and adds one.

### Retained pages

1. **Home** — evolved narrative (see homepage evolution above)
2. **Start Here** — updated to orient readers within the alignment framing
3. **Proof-of-Usefulness** — retained, reframed as a drift example
4. **Read the Framework** — retained, with updated introductory framing
5. **Challenge or Contribute** — retained, with expanded input types

### New page

6. **The Evidence** (working title)
   - Web presentation of the formation-document convergence finding
   - Alignment matrix summary
   - Source corpus overview
   - Links to full analysis artifacts

### Search and discovery

Phase 2 should include a dedicated on-site search experience for public documents:

- **Search page (`/search`)** with full-text query input and category filters (core, formation docs, memos, proposals, process, exchanges)
- Search results should include document title, excerpt, and source path so users can distinguish similarly named artifacts quickly
- Search should work without GitHub authentication and without introducing new backend infrastructure
- The implementation should remain compatible with the static export architecture (index generated at build time, served from S3 + CloudFront)

## Success Criteria

Phase 2 is successful if the alignment framing produces a qualitatively different response from visitors.

Qualitative indicators:

- visitors engage with the alignment thesis rather than treating the site as a standard policy platform
- the formation-document evidence is cited or referenced in external discussion of the project
- the "what if we're not as divided as we think?" framing draws engagement from people who would not have engaged with a reform-first framing
- serious challenges to the convergence claim surface (this is success, not failure)
- the politically-homeless audience recognizes something in the framing that they do not find elsewhere

Quantitative indicators (building on Phase 1):

- visits to the formation-document evidence page
- time on site and scroll depth on the homepage (does the new narrative hold attention?)
- response volume and quality compared to Phase 1 baseline
- diversity of respondent backgrounds (are new audience segments engaging?)

## Explicit Deferrals for Later Phases

These are important but should not block Phase 2:

- interactive formation-document explorer or comparative tool
- full internationalization
- additional proof-of-usefulness artifacts beyond the comparative memo
- video or multimedia presentation of the alignment thesis
- community features beyond the existing contribution path
- formal academic publication of the formation-document findings

## Relationship to Project 2028

Phase 2 depends on work being done in the `project-2028` repository:

- `formation-docs/ALIGNMENT_FRAMEWORK.md` — the rubric for the convergence analysis
- `formation-docs/analysis/synthesis/alignment-matrix.md` — the cross-source convergence data
- `formation-docs/analysis/synthesis/gap-analysis.md` — where the corpus challenges or extends the principles
- `formation-docs/analysis/synthesis/uniqueness-report.md` — what Civic Blueprint says that the corpus does not
- `formation-docs/analysis/principle-maps/` — the source-by-source evidence

The website should vendor or reference these artifacts. The formation-document evidence is the empirical backbone of the Phase 2 narrative, and the website cannot credibly present the alignment thesis without making that evidence accessible.
